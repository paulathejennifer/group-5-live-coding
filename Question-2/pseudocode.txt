Input
A list of patients that will  include information on the equipment they require,
A list of beds in the hospital, and their state, whether they are occupied or not,
A list of doctors and nurses, and what they do or their expertise, if they are free or busy, a list of special types of equipment, and if they are free or busy

Process
1. Review all the patients and place those who require the most assistance at the top of the list.

2. For each patient, one at a time find a free bed.

3. Find a free doctor or nurse who can help with that patient’s problem.

4. Find any special machines the patient needs, if they are free.

5. Make sure not to give the same bed, doctor, nurse, or machine to two people at the same time.

6. If you can find everything the patient needs, assign it to them and mark those things as busy.

7 . If you can’t, put the patient on a waiting list.

8. When a patient leaves or something becomes free, check if you can help someone from the waiting list now.

9 . If suddenly a lot of new patients come in, help the ones in the most danger first, even if others have to wait a little longer.

10. Try not to let any beds, staff, or machines sit unused if patients are waiting.

   Output
A list showing which patient is in which bed, with which doctor/nurse, and using which machines
A list of patients who are still waiting, with notes on what they still need summary showing how busy the hospital is warnings 
   If there are not enough people or machines to help everyone
